How Platform Engineering Creates Strategic Capacity

By Anas Semesmieh · March 15, 2026 · Platform Engineering

A common platform challenge is that teams become trapped in KTLO work while strategic delivery slows down. In practice, this creates a cycle of constant firefighting, fragmented ownership, and low confidence in release quality.

The most reliable way to break this cycle is to combine self-service, automation, and clear platform product ownership.

1. Reduce KTLO to reclaim engineering focus

One of our biggest goals was to reduce operational load that added little strategic value. Through workflow standardization, automated runbooks, and better platform guardrails, we reduced KTLO effort by 30 percent. That recovered capacity was redirected to architecture modernization and delivery acceleration.

2. Introduce an Internal Developer Portal

We introduced an IDP to simplify common tasks for product teams. Instead of waiting on platform engineers for every request, teams could use self-service workflows for provisioning, environment updates, and release-related actions. This improved developer experience and shortened lead time for change.

3. Expand automation across repetitive workflows

Automations were designed as products, not scripts. We targeted friction points that occurred frequently and had measurable impact on lead time, reliability, or security posture. This helped scale consistency as systems and teams grew.

4. Use AI-assisted workflows pragmatically

AI was used behind the scenes to increase execution speed, improve documentation quality, and accelerate analysis and implementation tasks. The important point was practical adoption: AI usage was promoted with clear patterns and guardrails so the team could use it responsibly and repeatedly.

Closing thought

Platform engineering delivers the most value when it is treated as an internal product that balances legacy constraints with modern architecture goals. The objective is not only reliability, but also strategic capacity.
